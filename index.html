Momentum Miami's 2025 Collective Impact Report credits designated programs with serving more than 300,000 Miami-Dade residents, more than 10% of the county's population. The report also says 73% of projects want more strategic partnerships and 63% want visibility and amplification. At the same time, several grantees (Senior Planet, Common Sense Media, Rebrand Cities) are actively teaching AI skills to the communities they serve. Those two facts, taken together, describe an opening.

The portfolio is becoming a network. AI is becoming the layer everyone wants to talk about. Those two conversations are not yet connected.

Where the 60/30/10 read comes in.

Our read on most AI conversations is that 60% of what gets called AI work is traditional code and database work. 30% is rule-based logic. The remaining 10% is where a language model genuinely earns its keep. Applied to a grantee network, that means a shared methodology for deciding which layer each piece of a program belongs on matters more than any specific tool. Grantees will adopt tools on their own timelines. A shared frame for deciding how to adopt them is the kind of thing a funder-consortium can actually provide.

A method small enough for a three-person grantee team to adopt.

Eduba has published one such frame. The Interpretable Context Methodology (ICM) is a method for organizing how AI systems carry their context (what they know, who they are, what they are working on) so that the result is auditable and reproducible. It was submitted to ACM TiiS and the working repo is on GitHub under MIT license. It is small enough that a three-person grantee team can adopt it and large enough to be useful across very different grantees.

For grantees working in regulated settings or across sensitive audiences, the Ethics Engine is a companion tool: a psychometric assessment tool for evaluating ideological and moral patterns in LLMs. Paper: arxiv.org/abs/2510.11742. Repo: github.com/RinDig/AuditEngine.

Precedent for what a shared enablement layer looks like at scale.

Correlation One ran a program with Pacific Life and Colgate-Palmolive that trained 1,500+ people since May 2025, saving 6,000 to 9,000 hours per year, with 95% of participants still using the tools 30 days later. Different audience, same shape: a shared enablement framework, delivered across multiple organizations, measured through outcomes rather than attendance.

KPMG UK, one of the Big Four, ran a parallel executive-level engagement with 40+ leaders. The common thread is the same one Momentum Miami's grantees report they want: shared methodology, not another vendor stack.

Eduba partners with NLP Logix for work that sits below the orchestration layer. NLP Logix has been in machine learning since 2011 and runs over 150 data scientists.
